# Tech Lead Review — Portfolio (Non-Technical, Critical)

## Straight Assessment
You have a strong foundation, but the portfolio is currently in a **"good designer"** position, not yet a **"high-value strategic leader"** position.

If your goal is to win better opportunities now, connect a blog, and later sell products, your main gap is:

- **Message clarity and trust architecture**, not visual quality.

---

## What Is Strong Today
- You already show a clear domain direction (strategic UX + operations-heavy products).
- You have real proof assets: case studies, outcomes, writing samples.
- The structure can scale to future content without redesigning from scratch.

---

## Critical Issues To Fix First

### 1) Your promise is not sharp enough
Right now, a visitor can still ask: “Are you a UI executor, UX generalist, or strategic business designer?”

**Why this matters:** high-quality clients/recruiters buy clarity first.

**Fix:** one repeated value proposition everywhere.

> Example: “I help healthcare and enterprise teams reduce operational friction through strategic UX systems.”

---

### 2) Case studies are still design-heavy, not decision-heavy
Many portfolios explain process. Fewer explain business decisions.

**Why this matters:** future product buyers and senior hiring managers look for judgment, not only screens.

**Fix each case study should clearly answer:**
1. What business risk existed?
2. What decision did you own?
3. What changed in operations?
4. What measurable outcome followed?

---

### 3) Blog is present, but not strategically connected
You have articles, but not yet a strong story loop between articles and case studies.

**Why this matters:** without internal linking, your content acts like isolated posts rather than a credibility engine.

**Fix:** every case study should link to 1 relevant article, and every article card should point back to a related case study.

---

### 4) Monetization path is not visible yet
You said products in 3–4 years. The portfolio does not yet show a path from content -> audience -> offer.

**Why this matters:** product success depends on trust + repeat audience long before launch.

**Fix now:** add lead capture + clear "work with me / learn with me" CTA.

---

## Domain + Hosting (Decision Guidance)

### Domain
Buy your personal brand domain now and keep it stable for years.

Recommended style:
- `firstname-lastname.com`
- plus one backup variation if affordable.

### Hosting
Pick for reliability and future expansion:
- **Vercel** if you plan product/app features later.
- **Netlify** if you prioritize fastest no-friction launch.
- **Cloudflare Pages** if you want edge speed and cost efficiency.

- Connect custom domain + hosting + analytics.

### Days 31–60

---

## Case Study Template You Should Standardize
Use this exact sequence for every major case study:
1. Business headline (result first)
2. Problem + business stakes
3. Your decision ownership
4. What changed in workflow
5. Outcomes (metrics)
6. Leadership reflection
7. Related article / next reading

This format is what moves you toward senior positioning and future product credibility.

---

## Final Tech Lead Recommendation
Do not spend the next month polishing visuals.

Spend it on:
- sharper positioning,
- stronger case-study decision narratives,
- content linking,
- and domain/hosting stability.

That combination creates a portfolio that can evolve from **job-winning asset** to **audience-building platform** to **product-selling foundation**.
